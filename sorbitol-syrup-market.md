<p><p>The sorbitol syrup market can be categorized into non-crystallising and crystallising types, each serving distinct purposes. Non-crystallising sorbitol syrup maintains a stable, viscous consistency, making it ideal for products requiring humidity control and sweetness without crystallization, such as candies and baked goods. In contrast, crystallising sorbitol syrup tends to form crystals, often used in applications where texture and appearance are essential, such as in confections and ice creams. Both types cater to specific industry needs and preferences.</p></p>
