<p><p>The antimycobacterial drugs market is categorized into three main types: Drugs used for tuberculosis (TB) primarily include first-line agents like isoniazid and rifampicin, crucial for TB treatment. Drugs used for leprosy, such as dapsone and rifampicin, target Mycobacterium leprae, focusing on effective management of the disease. Lastly, drugs for atypical mycobacteria, including macrolides like azithromycin and ethambutol, address infections caused by non-tuberculous mycobacteria, which can affect various populations, particularly those with compromised immune systems.</p></p>
