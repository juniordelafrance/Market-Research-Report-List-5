<p><p>The automotive all-season tire market consists primarily of two types: bias tires and radial tires. Bias tires feature layers of rubber that crisscross at angles, providing a sturdy but less flexible structure, ideal for specific utility vehicles. In contrast, radial tires have cords arranged at a right angle to the tread, offering better fuel efficiency, improved traction, and enhanced comfort, making them the preferred choice for passenger vehicles. Each type caters to distinct driving needs and consumer preferences in varying conditions.</p></p>
