<p><p>The PVB interlayers market is primarily divided into two types: Standard Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB) and Structural Polyvinyl Butyral. Standard PVB is widely used for laminated glass in applications such as automotive and architectural glazing, providing sound insulation and UV protection. In contrast, Structural PVB is engineered for enhanced strength and load-bearing properties, making it suitable for applications requiring increased safety and durability, such as in buildings and bullet-resistant glass. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing glass performance and safety.</p></p>
