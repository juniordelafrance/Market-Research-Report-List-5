<p><p>The automotive aftermarket for spark plugs includes hot and cold spark plugs, tailored for different engine performance needs. Hot spark plugs have a longer heat range, allowing them to retain more heat, making them ideal for low-performance engines and fuel efficiency. In contrast, cold spark plugs dissipate heat more efficiently, suitable for high-performance engines operating at elevated temperatures. Understanding the distinction between these types helps consumers choose the right spark plug to optimize engine performance and longevity.</p></p>
