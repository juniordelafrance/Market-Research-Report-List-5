<p><p>Dental acrylic materials are categorized primarily into opaque and transparent types, each serving distinct purposes in dental applications. Opaque acrylics are typically used for making prosthetics, dentures, and tooth replicas, as they closely mimic the appearance of natural teeth while providing essential coverage. In contrast, transparent acrylics are primarily utilized in applications requiring visibility, such as dental splints and temporary crowns. Both types offer excellent adhesion, durability, and aesthetic qualities, catering to diverse patient needs and enhancing dental treatments.</p></p>
