<p><p>The automotive aluminum alloy wheels market comprises three primary types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds to create intricate wheel designs, offering cost-efficiency for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping solid aluminum under high pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels, but typically at a higher cost. The "other" category includes processes like extrusion and machining, which cater to specialized applications or bespoke designs, further diversifying the market.</p></p>
