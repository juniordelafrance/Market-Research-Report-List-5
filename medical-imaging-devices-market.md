<p><ul><li>Computer Tomography (CT)</li><li>Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)</li><li>Positron Emission Tomography (PET)</li><li>Ultrasound</li><li>X-Ray</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical imaging devices market comprises various types of technologies used for diagnostic purposes. Computer Tomography (CT) utilizes X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images of the body. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) employs magnetic fields and radio waves to generate detailed images of organs and tissues. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) allows visualization of metabolic processes in the body. Ultrasound uses sound waves to produce images of soft tissues, while X-Ray captures images of bones and dense tissues. The "Others" category encompasses additional imaging modalities, such as fluoroscopy and mammography, broadening the diagnostic landscape.</p></p>
