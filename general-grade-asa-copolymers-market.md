Blow Molding involves creating hollow plastic parts by inflating a hot polymer into a mold, making it ideal for products like bottles and containers. Injection Molding, on the other hand, entails injecting molten polymer into molds to produce solid, complex shapes, commonly used for automotive and consumer goods components.
