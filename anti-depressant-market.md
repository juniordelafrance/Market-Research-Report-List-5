<p><p>The anti-depressant market comprises several key types based on their mechanisms. Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) block the reuptake of neurotransmitters, enhancing mood. Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) specifically target serotonin to alleviate depression symptoms. Serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs) affect both serotonin and norepinephrine, offering broader relief. Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) inhibit the breakdown of neurotransmitters, enhancing their effects. Serotonin antagonists and reuptake inhibitors (SARIs) target serotonin receptors while modifying reuptake processes. Additionally, "Others" encompass emerging treatments and alternative therapies.</p></p>
