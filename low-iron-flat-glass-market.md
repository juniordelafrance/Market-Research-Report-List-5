<p><p>Low iron flat glass is characterized by its high optical clarity and minimal green tint, making it ideal for applications requiring transparency, such as architecture and solar panels. The market primarily includes rolled glass, produced by passing molten glass between rollers, resulting in a smooth surface and uniform thickness. Float glass, on the other hand, is created by floating molten glass on molten tin, yielding a flawless surface and consistent quality. Both types serve various industries, including construction and automotive, due to their superior light transmission properties.</p></p>
